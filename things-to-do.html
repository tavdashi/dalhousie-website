Make the most of your trip

Things to Do

Viewpoints, meadows, forest trails, and quiet corners of colonial history.

Top Attractions

Dalhousie's appeal lies in its slower pace — most attractions are about soaking in views, walking through forest, or stepping into a piece of history. Here are the highlights worth building your itinerary around.

Khajjiar

A saucer-shaped meadow ringed by dense forest, often nicknamed "Mini Switzerland." A short drive from the town centre, it's popular for picnics, short walks, and horse rides across the open grass.

Dainkund Peak

The highest point near Dalhousie, reached via a short trek from Jot. On a clear day, the peak offers panoramic views of snow-covered ranges in every direction, making it a favourite for sunrise hikes.

St. John's Church

One of the oldest churches in Dalhousie, built in the 1860s. Its stone construction and stained-glass windows are a quiet reminder of the town's colonial-era past, set among tall pines.

Panchpula

A scenic spot named for the five bridges that once crossed its streams. Shaded walking paths and small waterfalls make it a relaxing stop, especially after the monsoon when the water flow is strongest.

Subhash Baoli

A natural spring surrounded by a dense pine grove, named after Subhash Chandra Bose who is said to have rested here. It's one of the cooler, quieter spots in town — ideal for a short forest walk.

Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary

A protected forest reserve home to deodar and oak trees, with a chance of spotting Himalayan black bear, leopard, and various bird species. Well-marked trails make it suitable for a half-day nature walk.

Activities

Beyond sightseeing, Dalhousie's forest trails and quiet roads make it a great place to slow down and get moving at your own pace.

Forest Trekking

Marked trails through Kalatop and toward Dainkund Peak suit both casual walkers and more experienced hikers.

Photography Walks

Misty mornings, colonial architecture, and mountain backdrops make Dalhousie a rewarding spot for photography.

Local Markets

Browse Gandhi Chowk and Subhash Chowk for woollens, Tibetan handicrafts, and local snacks.
