Getting there & getting around

Travel Guide

Plan your journey to Dalhousie and make the most of your time once you arrive.

How to Reach Dalhousie

Dalhousie is located in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, around 555 km from Delhi. It's well connected by road, with the nearest railway station and airport both a few hours' drive away.

By Air

The nearest airport is Gaggal Airport (Kangra), approximately 130 km away, with limited flights from Delhi. From there, taxis and shared cabs are available for the onward journey to Dalhousie, taking roughly 4–5 hours through hill roads.

By Rail

The closest broad-gauge railway station is Pathankot, around 80 km from Dalhousie, well connected to Delhi, Amritsar, and Jammu. From Pathankot, taxis and buses cover the remaining distance in about 3 hours.

By Road

Regular bus services run from Pathankot, Chamba, and Dharamshala to Dalhousie. Self-driving is popular too — the route via Pathankot offers scenic hill views, though winding roads mean the journey takes longer than the distance suggests.

Getting Around Locally

Once in Dalhousie, getting around is straightforward — most central attractions are walkable, while nearby spots like Khajjiar and Kalatop are easily reached by local taxi.

Walking

The town centre (Gandhi Chowk to Subhash Chowk) is compact and very walkable, with gentle uphill and downhill stretches connecting markets, viewpoints, and most budget accommodation.

Local Taxis

Shared and private taxis are the easiest way to reach Khajjiar, Kalatop, Dainkund Peak, and other attractions outside the town centre. Rates are usually fixed per route — agree on the fare before setting off.

Best Time to Travel

March–June and September–November offer the clearest roads and views. Travel during heavy monsoon (July–August) or peak winter snowfall (January) may involve road delays — check local conditions before setting out.
